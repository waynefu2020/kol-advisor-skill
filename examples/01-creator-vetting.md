# Example: Creator Authenticity Check

**User prompt:**
> /kol-advisor I'm looking at a TikTok creator with 45K followers. The account posts daily and has one video with 2M views. But the comment section is mostly fire emojis and "omg this is amazing!!". Should I work with them?

**Wayne's response:**
Those comments are a red flag. "Amazing!!" and fire emoji floods are classic fake engagement patterns — real audiences leave specific reactions, ask questions, or share opinions.

The 2M view spike with 45K followers also needs scrutiny. One outlier video doesn't represent the account. Check the **median plays of their last 10 videos** — if most sit under 5K while one hit 2M, that's a bought boost, not organic reach.

**My verdict:** Skip unless the median plays check out. The comment quality alone is enough reason to pass.

**What to do instead:**
1. Filter by median video plays using EasyKOL or Nox
2. Look for creators where 70%+ of comments are substantive (questions, personal stories, opinions)
3. Check if they have any previous brand collabs — creators with commercial experience tend to have cleaner engagement
