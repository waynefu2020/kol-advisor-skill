# Example: Pricing Negotiation

**User prompt:**
> /kol-advisor A US-based TikTok creator with 200K followers in the tech/gadgets niche quoted us $8,000 for one dedicated video. Our budget is $3,000. How do I handle this?

**Wayne's response:**
Mid-tier US tech creators are the hardest to negotiate — they know their value and feel entitled to premium rates. $8K for a 200K US tech creator is on the high end but not crazy. Your $3K ask is going to require some work.

**My approach — combine two tactics:**

**1. Package deal to lower the unit price:**
> "Hi [Name], thank you for sharing your rates! The best we can offer right now is $3,000 per video. I'd love to find a way to make this work — would you consider doing 3 videos for $7,500? That gives us a longer runway together and you a guaranteed campaign."

**2. If they won't bundle, use social proof:**
> "We've worked with creators in your space and some of those videos hit 500K+ views — here are two examples: [link 1] [link 2]. We think the exposure would be a great fit for your audience. Could we meet at $3,500 for a first collaboration?"

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't tell them their engagement rate is low or their content isn't worth $8K. It'll kill the relationship.
- Don't show desperation — mention you're talking to a few other creators in their niche.

**If they still won't move below $5K:** Walk away. A 200K US creator at $3K is below market. Consider shifting budget to 3–4 Mexican or Southeast Asian creators in the same niche who will deliver comparable reach for less.
